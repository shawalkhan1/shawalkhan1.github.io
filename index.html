Research Interests

I build AI systems that are not only intelligent but also fair, culturally aware, and genuinely useful for people. My research seeks to bridge advances in large language models, agentic AI, and Human-AI Interaction with real-world societal challenges, ensuring that AI technologies benefit diverse communities rather than a privileged few.

My work is organized around three research themes:

  • Trustworthy AI — evaluating and improving the fairness, safety, robustness, and reliability of foundation models through benchmarks, computational audits, and responsible evaluation frameworks.
  • Agentic & Interactive AI — designing intelligent agents that reason, collaborate, and adapt alongside humans across complex real-world tasks.
  • AI for Healthcare & Social Good — developing multilingual, culturally grounded AI systems that improve healthcare delivery, education, and public services in low-resource settings, with a particular focus on the Global South.

Overall, my research lies at the intersection of Human-AI Interaction (HAI), Responsible AI, Agentic AI, Healthcare AI, AI Engineering, and ICT for Development (ICTD).

Systems

Production LLM System

LLM Twin

An end-to-end system that learns a person's writing voice and answers as they would — the production blueprint behind SYNQ.

A multi-source ETL crawler pulls every digital trace (GitHub, Medium, personal sites) into a MongoDB warehouse. A Change-Data-Capture layer and a Bytewax streaming pipeline keep the twin's memory continuously fresh.
Text is cleaned, chunked, and embedded into a Qdrant vector database. The RAG layer uses query expansion, self-querying metadata filters, hybrid search, and a cross-encoder reranker.
Llama 3.1 8B fine-tuned with QLoRA/LoRA (TRL + Unsloth) on an instruction dataset, then aligned with DPO/RLHF. Runs are tracked and evaluated with LLM-as-a-judge and RAGAS in Comet ML.
Served as a real-time AWS SageMaker endpoint behind a FastAPI microservice with a LangGraph agentic-RAG brain. Optimized with quantization (GPTQ/AWQ/GGUF), speculative decoding, Flash Attention, KV-cache, and continuous batching; benchmarked across vLLM, TGI, and TensorRT-LLM.
Built on the Feature/Training/Inference (FTI) pattern, orchestrated by ZenML into deployable microservices, with CI/CD/CT via GitHub Actions, Opik observability, and drift detection that triggers automatic retraining.

SYNQ

Before people meet, their minds already have.

SYNQ is an AI compatibility and relationship-orchestration platform. Each user trains a high-fidelity AI Clone of themselves; these autonomous agents converse and probe values across the network, so a compatibility layer can surface high-probability human connections — across friendship, mentorship, hiring, and co-founder discovery — before a single human word is exchanged.

01 · Identity

The Clone

A behavioral model fusing a structured interview, psychometrics, and opt-in signals into a five-layer personality embedding: communication style, ethics, emotional intelligence, cognition, and social energy.

02 · Simulation

Autonomous Agents

Live LLM agents meet asynchronously through curiosity probes, value-negotiation dialogues, and conflict simulations — a persistent synthetic-social environment that surfaces authentic resonance.

03 · Intelligence

The Orchestrator

A meta-level engine that scores flow symmetry, sentiment trajectory, healthy debate, and vulnerability signals, then delivers a Compatibility Report only on bilateral consent.
