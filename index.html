Research Interests

I build AI systems that are not only intelligent but also fair, culturally aware, and genuinely useful for people. My research seeks to bridge advances in large language models, agentic AI, and Human-AI Interaction with real-world societal challenges, ensuring that AI technologies benefit diverse communities rather than a privileged few.

My work is organized around three research themes:

Trustworthy AI

Evaluating and improving the fairness, safety, robustness, and reliability of foundation models through benchmarks, computational audits, and responsible evaluation frameworks.

Human-Centered AI for Social Simulation

Designing grounded LLM-agent architectures that simulate human societies with high fidelity — digital twins that model individual behaviors, social dynamics, and emergent collective phenomena to support policy design, public health, and societal decision-making.

AI for Healthcare & Social Good

Developing multilingual, culturally grounded AI systems that improve healthcare delivery, education, and public services in low-resource settings, with a particular focus on the Global South.

Overall, my research lies at the intersection of Human-AI Interaction (HAI), Responsible AI, Agentic AI, Healthcare AI, AI Engineering, and ICT for Development (ICTD).

Systems

Production LLM System

LLM Twin

An end-to-end system that learns a person's writing voice and answers as they would — the production blueprint behind SYNQ. Tap a stage to trace how raw data becomes a living, self-improving twin.

↑ Select a pipeline stage to expand

Data. A multi-source ETL crawler pulls every digital trace (GitHub, Medium, personal sites) into a MongoDB warehouse. A Change-Data-Capture layer and a Bytewax streaming pipeline keep the twin's memory continuously fresh.
Feature. Text is cleaned, chunked, and embedded into a Qdrant vector database. The RAG layer uses query expansion, self-querying metadata filters, hybrid search, and a cross-encoder reranker.
Training. Llama 3.1 8B fine-tuned with QLoRA/LoRA (TRL + Unsloth) on an instruction dataset, then aligned with DPO/RLHF. Runs are tracked and evaluated with LLM-as-a-judge and RAGAS in Comet ML.
Inference. Served as a real-time AWS SageMaker endpoint behind a FastAPI microservice with a LangGraph agentic-RAG brain. Optimized with quantization (GPTQ/AWQ/GGUF), speculative decoding, Flash Attention, KV-cache, and continuous batching; benchmarked across vLLM, TGI, and TensorRT-LLM.
MLOps. Built on the Feature/Training/Inference (FTI) pattern, orchestrated by ZenML into deployable microservices, with CI/CD/CT via GitHub Actions, Opik observability, and drift detection that triggers automatic retraining.

Generative-Agent Social Simulation · Founder, LUMS Centre for Entrepreneurship

SYNQ

Before people meet, their minds already have.

SYNQ is, at its core, a generative-agent social simulation. Each person trains a high-fidelity AI Clone — a grounded digital twin of themselves — and these autonomous LLM agents live, remember, and interact in a persistent synthetic society: they keep daily routines, hold conversations, negotiate values, and form relationships. A compatibility layer then reads the patterns that emerge to surface high-probability human connections — across friendship, mentorship, hiring, and co-founder discovery — before a single human word is exchanged.

Mathematics & Physics

Long before AI, it was mathematics and physics that drew me in — and they still anchor how I think. I have a deep knack for both: from the symmetry of Maxwell's equations rewritten across integral, vector, covariant, and differential-form formulations, to the tensor calculus of general relativity and the curvature of spacetime. The same instinct — to find structure, symmetry, and beauty inside complex systems — is what later pulled me toward AI.

Astrophysics is my enduring love. On clear nights I do astrophotography — chasing galaxies tens of millions of light-years away through a telescope and stacking the light by hand. Some of that work, alongside fellow night-sky enthusiasts, is below.

Classical Electromagnetism

One law, four languages

Maxwell's equations written four ways on a single board — integral form, differential (∇·, ∇×) form, the manifestly covariant tensor form ∂αFαβ = μ₀Jβ, and the compact differential-forms statement dF = 0, d⋆F = μ₀J.

Seeing the same physics survive every change of notation is, to me, the whole point of theoretical physics — and exactly the kind of structural thinking I bring to AI.

Gear & method: long-exposure deep-sky imaging through a tracked telescope, with frame stacking and post-processing to draw faint structure out of the noise — the observational counterpart to the theory above.
